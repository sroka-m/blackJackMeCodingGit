How to play Blackjack

Below are rules used for this online version of the game.
Blackjack rules are explained in many articles and videos. The rules between casinos might vary slightly.

Card values
Blackjack is played with 52 cards: 4 suits, 13 cards in each suit. Cards 2 to 9 have values equal to their numbers, court cards are valued at 10 and aces are valued at 11 or 1, whichever is better for the person playing.
Main principle
The objective is to get as close to 21, without exceeding 21. No matter how many player are in the game (this version is limited to one player), each player plays only agains the dealer.
Blackjack (Natural)
Blackjack is when the player/dealer score exactly 21 from the first two cards. If both have 2 initial cards that sum to 21, they tie. If player wins as a result of blackjack they are paid 150% of their bet. It is worth highlihting that scoring 21 from any other cards than the first two is not Blackjack. Also scoring 21 from splitting is not Blackjack (see later).
If the dealer's face-up card is a ten-card or an ace, they look at their face-down card to see if the two cards make a Blackjack. If the face-up card is not a ten-card or an ace, they do not look at the face-down card until it is the dealer's turn to play.
The play
The player goes first. They need to decide whether to "stand" (not ask for another card) or "hit" (ask for another card in an attempt to get closer to a count of 21, or even hit 21 exactly). Thus, the player may stand on the two cards originally dealt to them, or they may ask the dealer for additional cards, one at a time, until deciding to stand on the total (if it is 21 or under), or goes "bust" (if it is over 21). In the latter case, the player loses and the dealer collects the bet wagered.Even if both dealer and player "bust" it is the player who loses.
The combination of an ace with a card other than a ten-card is known as a "soft hand," because the player can count the ace as a 1 or 11, and either draw cards or not. For example with a "soft 17" (an ace and a 6), the total is 7 or 17. While a count of 17 is a good hand, the player may wish to draw for a higher total. If the draw creates a bust hand by counting the ace as an 11, the player simply counts the ace as a 1 and continues playing by standing or "hitting" (asking the dealer for additional cards, one at a time).
The dealers play
After the player decided to stand, the dealers face-down card is turned up. If the total is 17 or more, it must stand. If the total is 16 or under, they must take a card. The dealer must continue to take cards until the total is 17 or more, at which point the dealer must stand. If the dealer has an ace, and counting it as 11 would bring the total to 17 or more (but not over 21), the dealer must count the ace as 11 and stand.
THe dealer cannot double down or split pairs.
Player strategy
There are 4 actions a player can take in this game: hit, stand, double down and split pair.(double down and split pair will be covered in later versions)
